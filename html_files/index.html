Some Tea-rrific facts!

Tea: The Original Paycheck!


Long before banknotes, China was out here paying salaries in tea—yep, actual tea bricks! Back in the Tang Dynasty, tea was so valuable it bought you horses, silk, and probably some serious bragging rights. Imagine paying your rent in tea today—your landlord might just evict you for being too “historical.”

Bangladesh Runs on Tea, Not Water!


Who needs water when you have endless cups of cha? In Bangladesh, tea is practically a national fuel—found in villages, cities, and every cha-er dokan (tea stall) in between. Whether you like it black, milky, or spiced, tea is the real MVP, turning strangers into friends one sip at a time!

Bangladesh’s Tea Can Wake the Dead


Forget mild and subtle—Bangladeshi tea is strong enough to kickstart your soul. Sylhet and Srimangal teas are packed with flavor, color, and enough caffeine to make coffee jealous. Add condensed milk and sugar, and you’ve got a drink so thick and rich, it should probably be taxed!
